Anterior cervical ACDF / TDA / Corpectomy

Anterior cervical procedures encompass ACDF (anterior cervical discectomy and fusion), cervical disc arthroplasty (total disc replacement), corpectomy, and associated instrumentation. CPT 22551 is the single most commonly billed anterior cervical code and was created in 2011 to replace the prior combination of 63075 + 22554 when discectomy and fusion are performed at the same level. This is a frequent source of coding confusion: 22551 and 22554 describe different procedures and are not interchangeable.

Key distinction: 22551 vs 22554

22551 = ACDF with discectomy, osteophytectomy, and decompression of spinal cord and/or nerve roots at the same level. This is the standard ACDF code when both discectomy and fusion are performed together. 22554 = anterior interbody fusion with minimal discectomy to prepare the interspace, without decompression. Used when only fusion is performed (no nerve root or cord decompression). Per NCCI edits, 22554 and 63075 (anterior cervical discectomy) cannot be reported together at the same level; use 22551 instead. However, if discectomy is performed at one level and fusion alone at a different level, 22551 and 22554 may both appear on the same claim with modifier 59.

NCCI edit pairs and bundling rules

22554 + 63075 at the same level: NEVER report together. Since 2011, ACDF at a single level is reported as 22551 only. Reporting 22554 + 63075 together at the same level is a bundling violation. Both codes still exist for situations where discectomy and fusion are performed at different levels.
22551 + 22554: Only with modifier 59 at different levels. If ACDF with decompression is performed at C5-6 (22551) and fusion alone without decompression at C6-7 (22554-59), both may be reported. Documentation must clearly state that decompression was not performed at the 22554 level.
22845 vs 22846: Segment count is vertebral bodies, not interspaces. A 2-level ACDF at C5-6 and C6-7 involves 3 vertebral segments (C5, C6, C7) with a plate spanning C5 to C7. Use 22845 (2-3 segments). A 3-level ACDF involves 4 vertebral segments, requiring 22846 (4-7 segments). Reporting 22845 for a 3-level ACDF is an undercoding error.
22853 vs 22854: Cage codes depend on the fusion context. 22853 = interbody biomechanical device placed with interbody arthrodesis (ACDF, TLIF, ALIF). 22854 = device placed with corpectomy defect reconstruction. Using 22854 when no corpectomy was performed is incorrect. For multi-level ACDF, report 22853 for the first interspace and 22854 for each additional interspace.

Frequently asked billing questions

Can I bill 22551 + 22554 on the same case?

Yes, but only if performed at different levels. For example, if ACDF with full decompression is done at C5-6 (22551) and fusion alone without decompression at C6-7 (22554-59), both codes are appropriate. You cannot bill both at the same level. If discectomy and fusion are performed together at the same level, use 22551 only.

For a 3-level ACDF, do I use 22845 or 22846 for instrumentation?

22846. Count vertebral segments, not interspaces. A 3-level ACDF (e.g., C4-5, C5-6, C6-7) spans 4 vertebral segments (C4 through C7). 22845 covers 2-3 segments, 22846 covers 4-7 segments. A 2-level ACDF spans 3 segments and uses 22845. This is one of the most common undercoding errors in cervical spine billing.

When should I use 22853 vs 22854 for the cage code?

22853 is for interbody biomechanical device (cage/spacer) placed with interbody fusion. 22854 is for device placed in a corpectomy defect. For a standard ACDF, use 22853 for the first interspace, then 22854 for each additional interspace. If a corpectomy was performed, use 22854 instead. These codes are frequently confused in multi-level cases.

How do I code a revision ACDF where prior hardware is removed and replaced?

There is no specific CPT code for "revision ACDF." Code the fusion itself (22551 if discectomy/decompression is performed, 22554 if not). If the prior instrumentation is removed and replaced at the same levels, report 22849 (reinsertion of spinal fixation device). If you are extending the fusion to new levels, 22849 does not apply; instead, use modifier 22 on the primary code and document the additional work and risk of operating through scar tissue. If the prior fusion is explored without performing a new fusion, use 22830 (exploration of spinal fusion).

Frequently asked billing questions

Can I bill laminoplasty (63050) with posterior fusion at the same levels?

Generally no. Laminoplasty is a motion-preserving procedure and should not be combined with fusion at the same levels. If laminoplasty is performed at some levels and fusion at others (e.g., laminoplasty C3-C5, fusion C5-C6 for instability), both may be reported with modifier 59 and clear documentation of distinct indications at distinct levels.

How do I code a C1-C2 screw fixation vs a sublaminar wire construct?

Both use 22595 (posterior fusion C1-C2) as the primary code. The instrumentation code depends on the construct: Goel-Harms C1 lateral mass + C2 pedicle/pars screws = 22840 (non-segmental, 1 interspace). Sublaminar wiring = 22841 (wiring of spinous processes, 0 wRVU). The screw fixation generates significantly more wRVU than wiring.

Anterior / lateral lumbar ALIF / LLIF / XLIF / OLIF / Lumbar TDA

Anterior and lateral lumbar interbody fusion codes cover ALIF (anterior lumbar interbody fusion), LLIF/XLIF/OLIF (lateral/extreme lateral/oblique lateral interbody fusion), lumbar total disc arthroplasty, and the AxiaLIF (pre-sacral approach). CPT 22558 is used for all of these procedures regardless of the specific approach variant. The key billing distinction is that lateral approaches (LLIF, XLIF, OLIF) are coded identically to ALIF using 22558; there is no separate CPT code for lateral approach. The approach surgeon (if a separate access surgeon is used for ALIF) bills with modifier 62 (co-surgeon).

Frequently asked billing questions

Is there a separate CPT code for XLIF vs ALIF vs OLIF?

No. All anterior and lateral lumbar interbody fusion approaches use 22558 for the first interspace and +22585 for each additional interspace, regardless of the specific surgical approach. The CPT system categorizes by anatomic result (anterior interbody fusion), not by the surgical corridor used to get there. DLIF, XLIF, LLIF, and OLIF are all coded as 22558.

How do I code a combined anterior-posterior (360-degree) fusion when done same-day?

If both the anterior interbody (22558) and posterior fusion (22612) are at the SAME level, use 22633 (combined posterior interbody + posterolateral fusion). If the anterior and posterior components are at DIFFERENT levels, bill 22558 and 22612 separately with modifier 59 on the lesser-valued code. For a same-day ALIF + posterior percutaneous screws at the same level, the question is whether posterolateral fusion (decortication + graft placement) was performed. If only screws were placed without posterolateral fusion, bill 22558 + 22840 (instrumentation only, no separate fusion code).

NCCI edit pairs and bundling rules

63047/63042 + 22630/22633 at the same interspace: CMS does NOT allow separate payment. This is the single most contested billing issue in spine surgery. CMS NCCI policy states: "CMS payment policy does not allow separate payment for CPT codes 63042 or 63047 with CPT codes 22630 or 22633 when performed at the same interspace." The rationale is that 22630/22633 includes "laminectomy and/or discectomy to prepare interspace." CPT Assistant has published conflicting guidance stating decompression beyond exposure may be separately reportable. However, CMS NCCI edits override CPT Assistant for Medicare claims. Commercial payers may follow CPT Assistant guidance. Always check the specific payer policy.
63047 + 22630/22633 at DIFFERENT interspaces: Reportable with modifier 59/XS. If decompression (63047) is performed at L3-4 and fusion (22633) at L4-5, both are separately reportable with modifier 59 appended to 63047. Documentation must clearly specify the distinct levels and distinct indications.
63047 + 22612 (posterolateral fusion, not interbody): Modifier 59/XS allowed. Unlike the 22630/22633 bundling issue, laminectomy (63047) may be separately reported with posterolateral fusion (22612) at the same level when the decompression is a distinct service performed for a separate indication (e.g., stenosis requiring decompression at the level being fused for instability). Use modifier 59 or XS. UHC and Aetna frequently deny even with the modifier; be prepared for peer-to-peer.
63030 (microdiscectomy) is bilateral by definition. CPT 63030 describes "hemilaminectomy" but the code value includes the work for unilateral or bilateral access. Do not report 63030 with modifier 50. If bilateral decompression is performed at the same level, it is still one unit of 63030.

Frequently asked billing questions

Can I bill decompression (63047) separately when I do a TLIF (22630/22633) at the same level?

For Medicare: No. CMS NCCI policy explicitly prohibits separate payment for 63042/63047 with 22630/22633 at the same interspace, even with modifier 59. The decompression required to access the disc space is considered included in the interbody fusion code. For commercial payers: Some follow CPT Assistant guidance, which states decompression beyond what is necessary for fusion access may be separately reported. Check each payer's NCCI policy. This is the single most litigated billing question in lumbar spine surgery.

What is the difference between 63005 and 63047?

63005 = laminectomy without facetectomy, foraminotomy, or discectomy (pure central decompression for stenosis). 63047 = laminectomy with facetectomy and foraminotomy (includes lateral recess and foraminal decompression). 63047 captures more work and is the appropriate code when any facetectomy or foraminotomy is performed in addition to central laminectomy. Most modern decompression procedures include some facetectomy, making 63047 the more commonly used code.

Can I bill modifier 50 on microdiscectomy 63030?

No. CPT 63030 inherently describes unilateral or bilateral decompression at a single interspace. The code value already accounts for bilateral work. Applying modifier 50 would be overcoding. If microdiscectomy is performed at two different interspaces, use 63030 + 63035 (add-on for each additional interspace).

Posterior TL fusion / deformity PLIF / TLIF / PLF / PSO / Revision

Posterior thoracolumbar fusion codes cover posterolateral fusion (22610-22614), posterior interbody fusion PLIF/TLIF (22630-22634), the combined code (22633-22634), and osteotomy/deformity correction codes (22210-22216). CPT 22633 is the most commonly billed code for a standard 1-level TLIF with posterolateral fusion. The choice between 22612 (posterolateral only), 22630 (interbody only), and 22633 (combined) depends on whether an interbody cage was placed and whether posterolateral fusion (decortication + graft over transverse processes) was also performed.

Key code selection logic

22612 = posterolateral fusion only (no interbody cage). Used when pedicle screws + rods are placed with posterolateral bone grafting but no interbody device. 22630 = posterior interbody fusion only (PLIF/TLIF with cage, but no posterolateral fusion). Rarely used in modern practice since most TLIF cases also include posterolateral fusion. 22633 = combined posterior interbody + posterolateral fusion (standard TLIF). The correct code when both an interbody cage and posterolateral bone grafting are performed at the same level. Do not report 22612 + 22630 at the same level; use 22633 instead.

NCCI edit pairs and bundling rules

22612 + 22630 at the same level: Use 22633 instead. 22633 was created specifically for cases where both posterolateral and posterior interbody fusion are performed at the same level. Reporting 22612 + 22630 at the same level is always incorrect. At different levels, both may be reported.
22630/22633 inherently include laminectomy for interspace preparation. The code descriptor states "including laminectomy and/or discectomy to prepare interspace (other than for decompression)." The laminectomy, facetectomy, and discectomy necessary to access the disc space for cage placement are included and cannot be separately billed at the same level. Only decompression at a DIFFERENT level than the fusion, or decompression that is clearly documented as beyond what is necessary for fusion access, may be separately reported.
Osteotomy codes (22210-22214) are primary codes, not add-ons. Unlike most spine procedure modifier codes, osteotomy codes carry their own wRVU values (20+ wRVU each) and are reported as primary procedures alongside the fusion code. They represent the additional work of bone removal for deformity correction. 22216 (each additional vertebra) is the only add-on in this series.

SI joint MIS Fusion / Open Fusion / Injection

Sacroiliac joint fusion has become an increasingly common procedure with the development of minimally invasive lateral transfixing implant systems. CPT 27279 describes the MIS approach (lateral transfixing implant, e.g., iFuse), while 27280 describes the traditional open posterior approach with bone graft and instrumentation. The MIS technique has a significantly lower wRVU (11.83 vs 19.50) reflecting the reduced operative complexity, but the procedural volume is substantially higher due to less restrictive indications and faster recovery.

NCCI edit pairs and bundling rules

27279 is unilateral. For bilateral SI joint fusion, report 27279 with modifier 50 or as two line items with RT/LT. Payer policies vary on bilateral same-day SI joint fusion. Some require modifier 50, others require two line items. Medicare generally covers bilateral same-day 27279. UHC and some commercial payers may require separate sessions for bilateral SI joint fusion.
SI joint injection (27096) for diagnostic confirmation is typically required before SI joint fusion authorization. Most payers require at least one documented diagnostic SI joint injection with >75% pain relief lasting at least the expected duration of the anesthetic agent before authorizing SI joint fusion. This is a pre-authorization requirement, not a coding issue, but missing the documentation of a positive diagnostic injection is a leading cause of SI joint fusion denials.
