2026 Update

Clinical context

Posterior and posterolateral fusion codes (22595-22614) represent the foundational billing codes for spine fusion surgery. These codes describe the arthrodesis procedure itself, which involves decortication of the transverse processes and facet joints, placement of bone graft material, and creation of a fusion bed. The approach (posterior or posterolateral) and the spinal region (cervical, thoracic, or lumbar) determine the specific code selection. CPT 22612 (lumbar posterolateral fusion, single interspace) is the highest-volume code in this category and the anchor code for most lumbar fusion cases.

All posterior fusion codes carry a 90-day global period, meaning routine postoperative care within 90 days of surgery is bundled and cannot be separately billed. Instrumentation (22840-22844), bone graft (20930-20938), and navigation (61783) are reported as separate add-on codes in addition to the primary fusion code. CMS applied a 2.5% efficiency adjustment to work RVUs for these procedural codes in 2026, reducing physician payments relative to 2025 despite the conversion factor increase.

Common add-on codes billed with posterior fusion

A typical posterior lumbar fusion case includes the primary fusion code plus instrumentation, bone graft, and potentially navigation. Each add-on code is reported separately. Bone graft codes (20930-20938) may each be reported once per operative session regardless of the number of levels fused.

NCCI edit pairs and bundling rules

22612 + 63005 (lumbar laminectomy): Modifier 59/XS required to unbundle. Laminectomy is bundled into the posterior fusion code when performed at the same level. To report both, the decompression must be documented as a separate, distinct procedure at a different anatomic level than the fusion, performed for a different pathology (e.g., stenosis at L3-4 separate from fusion at L4-5). Use modifier XS (separate structure) rather than 59 for lower audit risk.
22612 + 63047 (laminotomy/foraminotomy): Modifier 59/XS required. Foraminotomy at a non-fused level for foraminal stenosis distinct from the fusion pathology may be separately reported. Documentation must clearly specify the distinct site and separate clinical indication. BCBS frequently requires peer-to-peer for multi-level unbundling.
22840 and 22842 are mutually exclusive. Non-segmental instrumentation (22840, for single-interspace pedicle fixation) and segmental instrumentation (22842-22844, for multi-segment constructs) cannot both be reported. Select based on the number of segments instrumented.

Common denial patterns by payer

UnitedHealthcare

Multi-level fusion (3+ levels) denied without peer-to-peer pre-authorization

Estimated impact: $15,000-40,000 per case. Response: Initiate proactive peer-to-peer before surgery. Document failed conservative treatment timeline with specific interventions, dates, and outcomes. Include functional deficit scores (ODI, VAS).

BCBS (various plans)

Unbundling denial on decompression (63047) + fusion (22612) without modifier XS

Estimated impact: $2,000-4,000 per case. Response: Operative note must explicitly state distinct decompression site and separate pathology. Use modifier XS (preferred over 59) for anatomic site distinction.

Medicare

MRI older than 12 months at time of surgery

Estimated impact: Full case denial. Response: Confirm MRI date within 6-12 months of scheduled surgery during the preoperative pathway. Order updated MRI proactively if approaching the recency threshold.

Workers Compensation

Lumbar fusion without 6 months of documented conservative care

Estimated impact: $20,000-50,000 per case. Response: Document a minimum 6-month conservative treatment timeline with specific interventions (physical therapy dates, injection dates and outcomes, medication trials, and functional assessments at each interval).
